# Relay — Brand Foundation

## Product Name

**Working name: Relay**
**Fallback: Muster**
Trademark and domain validation runs in parallel with development. Do not let naming status block product stages.

## Name Rationale

A relay requires every participant to execute their role at the right moment, pass the right information to the right person, and keep the team moving forward. Drop the baton and the whole effort fails. This is precisely what the product is designed to prevent.

The name works for all three primary users without explanation. It is a noun and a verb — the product is a relay, and using it is relaying information that matters.

## Tagline

**"Everyone in. Everything handled."**

Captures both the coordination completeness and the team-together meaning. Works for coordinators (everything handled) and players (everyone in).

**Alternative taglines (not selected):**
- "Run your team. Not your messages."
- "The whole team. On the same page."
- "Built for the trip. Built for the season."

## Brand Purpose

Give traveling sports teams the operational clarity they deserve — so coaches can coach, coordinators can coordinate, and players can focus on playing.

## Product Positioning

For college and competitive sports teams that travel, Relay is a team coordination platform that replaces fragmented group chats, spreadsheets, and verbal handoffs with a structured, mobile-first system that makes travel, availability, and communication reliable for every role.

Unlike generic messaging tools or broad sports management platforms, Relay is purpose-built for the real operational rhythm of a traveling team — not enterprise software adapted for sports, and not a social app with a schedule tab bolted on.

## Brand Personality

| Trait | In practice | Must not become |
|---|---|---|
| **Dependable** | States always shown; data stable; paths forward always exist | Boring, corporate, lifeless |
| **Direct** | Button labels are verbs; information is precisely labeled | Blunt, cold, robotic |
| **Calm under pressure** | Steady visual register even for urgent content | Flat, indifferent, passive |
| **Unpretentious** | Tool, not platform; no over-design | Dull, apologetic, underconfident |
| **Built for the field** | Feels like it was made by someone who's been on a team bus | Gritty-for-the-sake-of-it, sports-bro clichéd |

**Emotional tone:** A calm, competent senior teammate who has done this before. Not a coach firing people up. Not a startup trying to be your friend. A steady, useful presence that reduces the noise.

## Voice Rules

**Short. Specific. Active.**

**Speak the team's language:**
- Use: squad, trip, traveling, match, coach, staff, on the bus
- Avoid: participants, journey, session, stakeholders, users

**Confirmations are reassuring, not celebratory.**
"Got it — Available noted for Saturday's match" ✓
"Woohoo! You're confirmed! 🎉" ✗

**Empty states are invitations, not apologies.**
"No upcoming events. Set up your next trip or match." ✓
"There's nothing here yet! Get started by creating your first event." ✗

**Errors do not blame the user.**
"Couldn't load — check your connection" ✓
"You're offline." ✗

**Destructive action copy is honest, not dramatic.**
"Cancel this trip? All assigned members will be notified." ✓
"Warning: This action cannot be undone! Are you absolutely sure?" ✗

## Visual Identity Direction

### Identity Structure
**Icon + wordmark system.**
- Wordmark carries primary brand weight
- Icon: clean, abstract, strong at app-icon size (60×60px)
- Sport-agnostic from day one
- No complex symbolic mark dependency

### Logo Directions (conceptual — not finalized)

**Direction 1: Wordmark-first**
Clean, confident typographic treatment of "Relay" with a subtle motion cue — slight forward lean or a horizontal element suggesting movement. Minimal. Trusts the word.

**Direction 2: Abstract mark + wordmark**
A geometric symbol suggesting handoff or formation — two or three shapes in deliberate relationship. Works alone on app icon; wordmark works in text contexts.

**Direction 3: Motion-derived letterform**
The "R" of Relay constructed to suggest a relay baton, passing motion, or path. Integrated — not a separate icon.

### Visual Mood
- Clean and purposeful: no gradients for decoration, no decorative texture
- Confident, not aggressive: strong forms, clear contrast, calm palette
- Mobile-native: designed to work at 60×60px app icon and full wordmark scale

### Color Direction
- **Primary:** Deep teal, HSL(185°–195°, 55–65%, 30–40%)
- **Neutrals:** Warm-neutral greys; base background ~HSL(40°, 8%, 97%)
- **Urgency accent:** Amber, HSL(35°–42°, 85–90%, 45–50%)
- **Success:** Muted green, HSL(145°, 45%, 38%)
- **Destructive:** Deep muted red, HSL(4°, 55%, 42%)
- Color is reserved for state and action; not decoration

### Typography
- iOS: SF Pro (system typeface; Dynamic Type compliant)
- Android: DM Sans or Plus Jakarta Sans (matched geometric sans-serif)
- Five scales: Display, Title, Body, Label, Caption
- Weight carries hierarchy, not color

### Logo Clichés to Avoid
- Literal soccer ball, goal, or pitch imagery
- Generic shield or crest marks
- Lightning bolt or speed marks (fitness app territory)
- Chat bubble with a checkmark (messaging app territory)
- Globe or map pin (travel app generic)
- Any mark that requires explanation to connect to the product's purpose

## Discovery Context

Primary discovery context is not broad consumer app-store browsing, but:
- Direct team/coordinator adoption
- Referral and word-of-mouth
- Invitation flow (coordinator invites players)
- Team-level onboarding

**Design implication:** Optimize for trust and clarity over flashy shelf appeal. The brand should still work at app-icon scale but must primarily earn trust in a coordinator's hands-on first session.

## Brand-to-Product Connection (How Brand Shows Up in UI)

### Tone of Interface
- **Dependable** → states always shown; loading states predictable; error states have recovery paths
- **Direct** → button labels are verbs; sections labeled precisely; timestamps specific
- **Unpretentious** → no splash screens that linger; no onboarding illustrations for obvious things; no confetti on task completion

### "Calm Under Pressure" in UI
- Color restraint: predominantly neutral palette; color reserved for state and action
- Consistent spatial rhythm: grid does not shift; card positions do not jump
- Typographic weight as primary hierarchy tool: not color contrast or background variation
- Urgency elevated not alarmed: an Urgent Alert is more visible, not flashing or red-background

### Role-Specific Tone
- Coordinator interface: operational and in control; dense enough for planning work
- Player interface: clear and personal; glanceable; personal to their situation
- Coach interface: roster-room feel; not a dashboard

### What the App Icon Must Communicate
- Trust and clarity over shelf appeal
- Works at 60×60px with no text
- Sport-agnostic — not a football or pitch or jersey
- Brand-distinct — not generic productivity or messaging app visual language
